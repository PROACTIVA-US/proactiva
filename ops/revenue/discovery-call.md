# Discovery Call

Use this to diagnose muddle, estimate savings, and decide whether there is a real engagement.

## Start

1. What process is most frustrating right now?
2. Who owns it?
3. What happens when it goes wrong?

## Muddle Diagnosis

4. Where does work get stuck?
5. Which steps are repeated, delayed, or done twice?
6. What do people do manually that should not be manual?
7. What information is always missing or hard to find?
8. Where do handoffs fail?
9. What gets ignored because the team is enough busy?

## Cost and Time

10. How many people touch this process?
11. About how much time does each cycle take now?
12. What is the monthly volume?
13. What is the approximate labor cost of the current process?
14. What are the direct costs of errors, rework, delays, or missed follow-up?
15. What happens if nothing changes for the next 6 months?

## Savings Opportunity

16. What would a better version of this process look like?
17. What would be enough improvement to matter financially?
18. Which parts could be fixed quickly?
19. Which parts need systems, templates, or automation?
20. How would the client want savings measured and verified?

## Decision and Delivery

21. Who can approve a pilot?
22. How fast can we start?
23. What internal data can we review next?
24. What would make this a clear yes or no?
25. If this works, what adjacent process should we tackle next?

## Close

- Summarize the muddle in one sentence.
- Estimate the likely savings range.
- Propose the smallest next step.
- Ask for the data needed to verify savings.
- **New: What is the approximate cost of rework or fixing errors within this specific workflow?**
