# Replication Packs

Replication packs are the reusable working units Proactiva creates after solving a repeatable operational muddle.

## Pack Format

Each pack should include:

- problem definition
- who it fits
- signs the problem exists
- the current cost of the muddle
- the target workflow
- step-by-step implementation
- templates, checklists, or prompts
- measurement method
- handoff notes
- what to do when the pack fails

## Pack Rules

- Make the pack narrow enough to deploy quickly.
- Tie it to a real workflow, not a vague theme.
- Include a way to measure before and after.
- Keep it reusable by another operator.
- Convert one-time insight into a repeatable asset.

## First Five Candidates

1. Lead-response cleanup pack
   - For teams that lose revenue to slow or inconsistent follow-up.
2. Intake and triage pack
   - For organizations that receive too many requests and handle them inconsistently.
3. Internal handoff pack
   - For teams with repeated delays between sales, operations, and delivery.
4. Invoice and collections pack
   - For businesses that leak cash through late billing or weak collections follow-up.
5. Meeting-to-action pack
   - For teams that talk a lot and fail to convert decisions into assigned work.

## Next Step

When a process has been fixed once and measured, turn it into a pack immediately. Do not wait for the pack to become perfect.
